Problem Statement

Why SDAVS Matters?

🧩

Complexity

Satellite data is inherently complex, high-dimensional, and difficult to analyze for non-experts without specialized software.

🚀

Lack of Integration

No single platform provides real-time environmental monitoring, 3D visualization, and ML-based prediction in one simple interface.

⏱️

Speed & Accessibility

Traditional Earth observation tools are slow, expensive, and require significant hardware resources to process large imagery.

Introduction

Project Vision & Scope

💡

The Aim

Develop an intelligent system that analyzes satellite data to monitor vegetation, track natural disasters, and provide actionable visual insights.

⚡

Automation

Automate complex image processing tasks like spectral band extraction and NDVI calculation through a web-based interface.

🌾

Domain Impact

Support sustainable agriculture, forest conservation, and rapid disaster management using real-time satellite intelligence.

Methodology

Implementation Roadmap

1
Data Collection: Collect satellite data from Sentinel-2 and disaster data from NASA EONET.
2
Image Preprocessing: Clean, resize images and extract important spectral bands.
3
Geo Mapping: Map image pixels with geographic coordinates.
4
NDVI Calculation: Calculate NDVI to analyze vegetation health.
5
Vegetation Classification: Classify areas based on NDVI values.
6
Change Detection: Detect land and vegetation changes over time.
7
Disaster Monitoring: Integrate real-time disaster data.
8
Visualization & Storage: Display results on maps and store them in a database.
